About Me

My background and professional development were largely dedicated to the study of strength & conditioning and rehabilitation. While studying physical therapy, I found that I really enjoyed practicing critical thinking, problem-solving, and helping others in the process. However, due to unforeseen circumstances, I was forced to withdraw from university.

Fortunately, this gave me permission to explore other interests and the opportunity to reflect on my life's passions, goals, and aspirations. I realized that my interest in coding could lead to a fulfilling career, with more freedom to pursue my hobbies and entrepreneurial aspirations. Furthermore, so many of the hard & soft skills I gained as an aspiring physical therapist parallel those required of a software engineer!

For those reasons, I made the commitment and enrolled in Thinkful's software engineering course. I am so excited to learn, grow, and develop over the next few months!

A big part of who I am revolves around the lifelong pursuit of personal development. What started as a hobby, trying to emulate the habits and behaviors of those I admired, and eventually became ingrained in my life. Outside of personal development, I enjoy spending my leisure time pursuing my hobbies:

  • Hiking
  • Lifting
  • Wrestling
  • Traveling
  • Video Games

Local Library

This project makes retrieving library information on accounts, books, and authors more accessible. I used the following JavaScript methods to extract pertinent data from objects: find(), filter(), map(), some(), every(), and reduce().

Decoder Ring

This project allows users to encode or decode text using Caesar's Shift, Polybius Square, and Substitution Cipher. I used Test Driven Development to produce code, develop unit tests with Mocha & Chai, and handle errors.
